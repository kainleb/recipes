CZB-v10

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Ziti (or Penne)
  • 1.5 lbs Boneless skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized medallions
  • 2 large heads of Broccoli, cut into small florets
  • 4-6 cloves Garlic, minced (don't be shy)
  • 1/2 cup Dry white wine (Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc)
  • 1 cup Chicken stock (low sodium)
  • 6 tbsp Unsalted butter, cut into cubes (keep cold)
  • 1/2 cup Freshly grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan (plus more for serving)
  • Red pepper flakes, salt, and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup starchy pasta water (reserved)

Instructions

  1. Prep the Pasta & Broccoli: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Drop the ziti. Three minutes before the pasta is al dente, toss the broccoli florets directly into the boiling water with the pasta.
  2. Sear the Chicken: While the pasta cooks, heat a large skillet with a splash of olive oil over medium-high heat. Season chicken with salt and pepper and sear until golden and cooked through. Remove chicken from the pan and set aside.
  3. Build the Base: In that same pan, turn heat to medium and add a tablespoon of butter. Saute the garlic and a pinch of red pepper flakes for about 1 minute (until fragrant, not brown).
  4. Deglaze: Pour in the white wine to scrape up the brown bits (fond) from the chicken. Let it reduce by half. Add the chicken stock and bring to a simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  5. The "Melt" Technique: Turn the heat to low. Whisk in the remaining cold butter one cube at a time. This creates an emulsion rather than just "oily" melted butter.
  6. Toss and Emulsify: Drain the pasta and broccoli (remembering to save some pasta water!). Add them and the chicken into the skillet. Toss vigorously while sprinkling in the cheese.
  7. Pro Tip: If it looks too dry, add a splash of the reserved pasta water. The starch in the water combines with the cheese and butter to create that velvety "melt" sauce.
